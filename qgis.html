Populous Metropolitan Areas

This is a map created in QGIS showing the most populous metropolitan areas of the world

November 13, 2022 Faizal Jamal

The map showing the most populous metropolitan areas of the world over a light gray basemap.
This map uses the projection ESRI 54030 - World Robinson
The symbology used is Graduated Symbology
Graduated symbology type allows you to break down the data in a column in unique classes and choose a different style for each of the classes
The modes use different statistical algorithms to break down the data into separate classes.
For this method, I used the Pretty Breaks mode in QGIS.
Pretty Breaks is based on the statistical package Râ€™s pretty algorithm. It is a bit complex, but the pretty in the name means it creates class boundaries that are round numbers.
Reference: Chapter 02, QGIS Map Design, Locate Press.

Choropleth Map

Choropleth map illustrating the per capita gross domestic product (GDP) of the countries of the world in 2016.

October 19, 2020 Faizal Jamal

The map showing the per capita gross domestic product (GDP) of the countries of the world during 2016.
This map uses Equal Earth Projection. Equal Earth Projection is intended for making world maps.
Equal Earth is a pseudocylindric projection. All parallels, the central meridian, and both poles are presented as straight lines. The parallels are unequally spaced. The meridians are equally spaced and other than the central meridian are curved outward. The poles are 0.59247 times the length of the equator. The graticule is symmetric across the equator and the central meridian.

The symbology used is Rule Based.
I have used five classes to categorize the countries.

Choropleths are a type of thematic map in which areas are shaded or patterned in proportion to a numerical attribute representing a rate or ratio, such as population density or per capita income.
Reference: Chapter 03, QGIS Map Design, Locate Press.
